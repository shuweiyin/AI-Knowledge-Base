## 🎯 Project Goal

**What students will build:**
A personal AI Learning Wiki in Obsidian — with at least 5 linked notes on AI topics, a working frontmatter system, and Claude actively helping them expand their knowledge.

**What students will learn:**
- How to set up Obsidian and create a structured vault
- How to install and use the [[Claudian plugin]] to bring AI into their notes
- How to write connected notes using wikilinks
- How asking AI good questions produces better results
- How a personal knowledge base grows more valuable over time

## 📦 Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1 — Set Up Your Vault *(~30 minutes)*

**What to do:**
Get Obsidian installed and your vault structure in place.

1. Download and install [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) (free)
2. Create a new vault — name it something personal like `My-AI-Wiki` or `[YourName]-AI-Notes`
3. Create these folders inside your vault:
   ```
   01-AI-Concepts/
   02-Projects/
   03-Resources/
   ```
4. Create your first note: `Claude.md` in the vault root
5. In `Claude.md`, write 3 things:
   - Who you are and what grade you're in
   - What AI topics you're most curious about
   - What you want this wiki to help you learn

**Expected result:** A vault with a folder structure and a `Claude.md` file explaining your learning goals.

**Common mistake:** Naming folders with spaces and special characters → **Fix:** Use hyphens instead (e.g., `AI-Concepts` not `AI Concepts`)

---

### Step 2 — Install the Claudian Plugin *(~20 minutes)*

**What to do:**
Connect Claude AI to your Obsidian vault using the Claudian plugin.

1. In Obsidian, open **Settings** → **Community Plugins** → turn off Safe Mode
2. Click **Browse** and search for the plugin
3. Follow the setup instructions in [[Claudian plugin]] — you'll need to paste in an API key
4. Once installed, open the Claudian panel (look for the icon in the left sidebar)
5. Test it: type *"Hello! Read my Claude.md and tell me what topics I should start with."*

**Expected result:** Claude responds inside Obsidian, referencing something from your `Claude.md`

**Common mistake:** Plugin not responding → **Fix:** Check that your API key is entered correctly in plugin settings

> 📎 Full setup guide: [[Claudian plugin]]

---

### Step 3 — Write Your First 5 Notes *(~45 minutes)*

**What to do:**
Create 5 notes on AI topics you find interesting. Each note must follow the format below.

**Note template (copy this for each note):**
```markdown
---
title: "Your Topic Name"
tags: [AI, topic-name]
created: YYYY-MM-DD
---

# Your Topic Name

## What is it?
[2-3 sentences in your own words]

## Why does it matter?
[1-2 sentences — connect to real life]

## One analogy
[Compare it to something you already know]

## Related topics
[[Topic 1]] | [[Topic 2]]
```

**Suggested starter topics** (pick 5):
- `What is Artificial Intelligence`
- `Machine Learning`
- `Neural Networks`
- `AI in Everyday Life`
- `AI Ethics`
- `Natural Language Processing`
- `What is Obsidian` ← already in this vault! See [[What is Obsidian - Concept Note]]

**Expected result:** 5 notes, each with frontmatter, a short explanation, and at least 1 wikilink to another note

**Tip:** *Don't try to write perfectly — start rough. You'll use Claude to improve them in Step 4.*

**Checkpoint screenshot:** Take a screenshot of your graph view showing the 5 notes connected.

---

### Step 4 — Use Claude to Improve Your Notes *(~30 minutes)*

**What to do:**
Use the Claudian plugin to expand, improve, and link your notes with Claude's help.

1. Open your weakest/shortest note
2. Ask Claude: *"Read this note and help me improve the explanation. Keep it simple enough for a middle schooler."*
3. Review Claude's suggestion — **don't just copy-paste!** Read it, edit it, make it yours
4. Ask Claude: *"What wikilinks am I missing in this note?"*
5. Add the suggested links (create new stub notes if needed)
6. Repeat for at least 2 more notes

**Expected result:** At least 3 notes improved with Claude's help, with more wikilinks added

**Tip:** *The best prompts are specific. Instead of "improve this note," try "explain neural networks using a cooking analogy that a 12-year-old would get."*

---

### Step 5 — View the Graph & Reflect *(~20 minutes)*

**What to do:**
Open Graph View to see your knowledge web — then write a reflection.

1. Click the **Graph View** icon in Obsidian's left sidebar
2. Zoom out to see all your notes as nodes with connecting lines
3. Look for **orphan notes** (dots with no lines) — those need more links!
4. Add at least 1 more wikilink to any orphan notes you find
5. In a new note called `Wiki Reflection`, answer the reflection questions below

**Reflect — write your answers in `Wiki Reflection`:**
1. *What does your graph look like? Describe the shape of your knowledge web.*
2. *Which note are you most proud of and why?*
3. *What was the most surprising thing Claude helped you understand?*
4. *What topic do you want to add to your wiki next?*

**Expected result:** A complete, connected graph with no orphan notes and a written reflection.

## 🤝 Ethics Checkpoint

Before submitting, discuss with a partner:

- When Claude helped improve your notes, **how did you decide what to keep vs. change?**
- Is the knowledge in your wiki *yours* if Claude helped write it? What makes it yours?
- What would you **not** want to put in a note that Claude can read? (Think: privacy)

*Write a 2–3 sentence response to any one of these questions in your `Wiki Reflection` note.*
