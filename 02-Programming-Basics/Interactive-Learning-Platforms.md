# 🎮 Interactive Learning Platforms

> **Hook:** What if you could learn to code by making music, throwing a dance party, or exploring AI superpowers — all in your web browser, no downloads needed?

These two platforms are perfect for **beginners** who want to understand how coding and AI work through hands-on, game-like activities. No prior experience required!

---

## 🎯 What You'll Learn

By exploring these platforms, you'll be able to:
- Write your first lines of code using visual block-based programming
- Understand how sequences, loops, and events work in coding
- See how AI and [[Big Data]] are used in the real world
- Build something fun you can share with friends and family

**Prerequisite:** None — just curiosity!

---

## 1. 🎵 Code.org — Learn to Code Through Play

**Website:** [code.org](https://code.org)

[[Code.org]] is a free website packed with coding lessons for students of all ages. It uses a visual, block-based approach (drag-and-drop code blocks) so you can focus on *thinking like a programmer* before worrying about typing.

### 📚 Our Class Lessons

We use three specific Code.org activities. Your teacher will give you a class link — enter your **Section Code** to join the right class.

| Detail | Info |

## 2. 🔬 Google AI Quests — Explore AI & Big Data

**Website:** [research.google/ai-quests](https://research.google/ai-quests/intl/en_gb)

[[Google AI Quests]] is a collection of interactive missions created by Google Research. Each Quest is a self-guided journey into a real AI or [[Big Data]] topic — with short readings, visuals, and hands-on mini-activities.

### 🗺️ Why Google AI Quests?

Unlike Code.org (which focuses on *writing* code), Google AI Quests focus on *understanding* how AI systems work at a bigger picture level. You'll explore questions like:

- How does Google Translate understand 100+ languages?
- How does AI find patterns in millions of data points?
- What is [[Big Data]] and why does it matter for AI?
- How do AI systems learn to recognize images?

### 🚀 Getting Started

1. Open [research.google/ai-quests](https://research.google/ai-quests/intl/en_gb) in your browser
2. Browse the available Quests — pick one that sounds interesting to you!
3. Work through the Quest at your own pace — most take 15–30 minutes
4. Take notes in your vault using a [[Daily Note]] as you go

### 🏆 Recommended Quests to Start With

| Quest Theme | What You'll Explore |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **AI & Data** | How massive datasets are collected, cleaned, and used to train AI |
| **Machine Learning** | How computers learn patterns without being explicitly programmed |
| **Language & AI** | How AI understands and generates human language |

> 📝 **Tip:** After each Quest, write a brief reflection in your daily note — what surprised you? What questions do you still have?

## 🔗 How These Two Platforms Connect

| | Code.org | Google AI Quests |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| **Focus** | Writing code (programming concepts) | Understanding AI systems (big-picture concepts) |
| **Style** | Interactive coding editor, game-like | Reading + visual explainers + mini activities |
| **Best for** | "How do I build it?" | "How does it work?" |
| **Level** | Beginner | Beginner–Intermediate |

Together, they give you both the **hands-on skills** (Code.org) and the **conceptual understanding** (Google AI Quests) you need to be AI-literate.
