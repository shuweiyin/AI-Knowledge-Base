# What is Obsidian — Concept Note

## ⚡ Core Concept (One Sentence)

> Obsidian is a **free note-taking app** where every note is a plain text file stored on your computer — and you can link notes together like a personal Wikipedia.

---

## 🤔 Why It Matters

Most students keep notes in a notebook or Google Docs — and then never find them again. Obsidian is different: it helps you **build connections between ideas**, so your notes become a living knowledge base that grows smarter over time. For learning AI, having a well-organized second brain is as important as the learning itself.

---

## 📖 Detailed Explanation

### Part 1 — The Basics

Obsidian is a **Markdown-based note-taking app**. "Markdown" just means notes are plain text files with simple formatting symbols (like `**bold**` or `# Heading`) — no fancy proprietary format, no lock-in. Your notes are files on *your* computer, not on someone else's server.

When you open Obsidian for the first time, you create a **vault** — think of it as a folder that holds all your notes. Everything inside that folder is your knowledge base.

### Part 2 — How It Works (Step by Step)

1. **You write a note** in Markdown — it's saved as a `.md` file on your computer
2. **You add links** using double brackets: `[[Another Note Name]]` — this creates a clickable connection between notes
3. **Obsidian maps those links** into a visual **Graph View** — a web of dots and lines showing how all your notes connect
4. **You add metadata** (tags, dates, status) at the top of each note using **frontmatter** — this lets you filter and search notes powerfully
5. **Plugins extend Obsidian** — the community has built hundreds of add-ons for calendar views, databases, AI assistants, and more

### Part 3 — Real-World Applications

- **Students** use it to build a personal wiki of everything they learn in a course
- **Writers** use it to organize research, characters, and plot outlines
- **Researchers** use it to link papers, ideas, and experiments
- **Teachers** (like you!) use it to build curriculum knowledge bases — exactly what this vault is

## 🪄 Analogies & Stories

**Analogy 1 — The Spider Web 🕷️**
Your brain doesn't store facts in a list — it stores them in a *web*. When you think of "dog," you instantly connect to fur, barking, your neighbor's golden retriever, and that viral video. Obsidian works the same way: every note is a node, every `[[link]]` is a thread. The more threads, the stronger the web.

**Analogy 2 — Personal Wikipedia 📚**
Wikipedia is powerful not because of any single article, but because every article links to dozens of others. Obsidian lets you build your *own* Wikipedia — about AI, or anything else — where YOU decide what connects to what.

## ❓ Common Student Questions

**Q: Is Obsidian free?**
A: Yes — the core app is completely free. There are optional paid features (like Obsidian Publish for sharing online and Obsidian Sync for cloud backup), but everything you need for this class is free.

**Q: What's the difference between Obsidian and Google Docs or Notion?**
A: Google Docs is great for single documents. Notion is a database/wiki tool. Obsidian is uniquely focused on *linking ideas together* and keeping your data local (on your device, not in the cloud). Think of it as the tool built for *thinking*, not just writing.

**Q: Do I need to know how to code to use Obsidian?**
A: No! Basic Obsidian requires zero coding. You write normal text with a few simple symbols. Over time, you can explore more advanced features — but the basics are very beginner-friendly.

## ⚠️ Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | The Truth |
|--------------|-----------|
| "Obsidian is just another notes app" | It's a *knowledge graph* tool — the linking and graph view make it fundamentally different from apps like Apple Notes |
| "You need to be technical to use it" | Basic use is as simple as typing. Advanced features are optional |
| "My notes are stored online" | By default, Obsidian stores everything **locally on your device** — nothing goes to a server without your permission |
| "You have to write perfectly the first time" | Obsidian is built for evolving notes — start rough, refine over time |

## 🔑 Key Terms & Definitions

- **[[Vault]]**: The main folder that holds all your Obsidian notes — your entire knowledge base lives here
- **[[Wikilink]]**: A link created with `[[double brackets]]` that connects two notes — the foundation of Obsidian's power
- **[[Frontmatter]]**: YAML metadata at the very top of a note (title, tags, date) that helps organize and filter notes
- **[[Graph View]]**: A visual map showing all your notes as dots and all wikilinks as connecting lines
- **[[Plugin]]**: An add-on that extends Obsidian's features — like the Claudian plugin that connects AI to your vault
- **[[Markdown]]**: A simple text formatting language — `**bold**`, `*italic*`, `# Heading` — used for all Obsidian notes

## 🖐️ Try It — Quick Activity

**Activity:** Explore Your Graph View
**Time:** 5 minutes
**Materials:** Obsidian (already installed)

**Instructions:**
1. Open your Obsidian vault
2. Create two new notes: one called `Cat` and one called `Dog`
3. In the `Cat` note, type: `Cats and [[Dog]]s are both popular pets.`
4. Click the graph view icon (left sidebar) — you'll see `Cat` and `Dog` connected by a line
5. Add a third note called `Pet` and link both to it — watch the web grow!

**Reflect:** *"If this were your AI knowledge base, what 3 topics would you want to connect first — and why?"*

## 🤝 Ethics Connection

Obsidian stores your notes **locally** — this is a strong privacy feature. Unlike cloud-based tools, your personal thoughts, research, and work never leave your device unless you choose to share them. As you learn about AI and start using AI tools *inside* Obsidian (like the Claudian plugin), always be mindful of what data you share with external AI services and what you keep private.
